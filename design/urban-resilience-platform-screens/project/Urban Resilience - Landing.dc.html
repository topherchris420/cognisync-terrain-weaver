A single, honest number
51 / 100 · Copenhagen
Moderate flood risk Vulnerable — but recoverable

The Urban Absorption Score fuses five land-cover classes into one figure between zero and one hundred, weighted by the runoff coefficients hydrologists have used for a century. It is not a black box: vegetation counts fully, bare soil nearly so, open water at half, and every impervious roof and roadway at almost nothing. A city that scores fifty-one is a city that sends half its rainfall into the drains — and half of that is a design decision we can reverse.

i.

Ground remembers

Soil and canopy hold a storm and release it slowly. Concrete cannot. Every square metre we soften is capacity we hand back to the watershed.

ii.

Measure to act

A defensible score turns intuition into a funded line item. Show a policymaker the number, then show them the number after the intervention.

iii.

Return, don't retreat

Green roofs, daylighted streams, permeable streets — resilience is not a wall against nature but an invitation back in.

The argument

Give the water somewhere to go.

For a century we engineered cities to shed rain as fast as possible. The storms have outgrown the pipes. The remedy is older than the problem: soften the ground, and let the city drink again.

This index scores a place on how much it can still absorb — and how far it has to travel back toward nature.
